About Us

Back in 2010, Brendan began his running classes in the Mall Sports Complex, Longford, as a couch-to-5K program organized by Longford Sports Partnership. After the six-week program concluded, most participants wanted to keep up the trainig, leading to the creation of Brendan Doyle Running Club. Some of those early runners are still with the club today, and as the classes grew in size, Madeline stepped in to assist with the beginners. Today, the club offers everything from couch-to-5K to full marathon training, and everything in between (and beyond). While most of their classes align with the school year, there is one class that runs year-round for more advanced runners. Also, every Saturday morning, they organise a long run that can have up to 5 different pace groups. There's a distance and pace to suit everyone.

Brendan started his own running journey in 1996 when he was living in New York. He started running to lose weight, and in 1998 he completed his first marathon in New York City. He ran it again in 1999, just before moving home to Ireland. To date, he has approximately 70 marathons under his belt, multiple half marathons, Ultra Marathons and Triathalons. In 2012 he ran from Sligo to Dublin for charity which consisted of five consecutive marathons totaling 210km. He did his first Ironman in Nice in 2009, and since then has completed five full Ironman and one double Ironman in 2017. He did 'The Race' in Donegal twice, which is Ireland's longest endurance race consisting of 250km in 24 hours. He has also qualified for the Ironman world championships in Kona in 2022. In 2015 they both started the Royal Canal Run in Longford. It’s races include 10k, half marathon, full marathon, and ultra marathon. It has grown over the years and sells out every year with a maximum of 850 runners.

Madeline started Running in 2010. Her first half marathon was part of the 32 Marathon Challenge with Gerry Duffy and Ken Whitelaw. She took part in the Longford leg, and in 2013 she entered the Dublin marathon for the first time, and again in 2016 and 2019. She ran the New York City marathon in 2018 and the Three Countries Marathon in 2022. She has completed multiple half marathons, 10k and 5k races. She has taken part in many sprint triathlons and an Olympic Triathlon. Madeline and her three children have taken part in Ireland's Fittest Family in 2020.
